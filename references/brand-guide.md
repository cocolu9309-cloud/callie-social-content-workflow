# Callie Brand Guide

Use this guide as the default Callie brand context when the user does not provide more specific campaign guidance.

## Brand Role

Callie.com is a personalized gift brand. Treat the brand as a place for thoughtful, custom, occasion-led gifts that help people express care through names, dates, memories, symbols, messages, and personal details.

## Core Categories

- Personalized jewelry
- Bags and accessories
- Birthday gifts
- Graduation gifts
- Wedding and bridesmaid gifts
- Mother's Day and Father's Day gifts
- Anniversary gifts
- Keepsake and memorial-style gifts when handled with care
- Occasion-led gift guides

Do not invent exact product specifications. If a product detail is not supplied by the user, refer to the category rather than the feature.

## Audience

Default to multi-country English-speaking gift buyers:

- People buying for partners, friends, parents, children, bridesmaids, graduates, coworkers, and family.
- Users who want a gift to feel personal, not generic.
- Shoppers looking for a sentimental, aesthetic, and useful gift.
- Social users looking for ideas, moodboards, reveal moments, and emotionally resonant stories.

## Positioning

Callie helps everyday gift-givers make important moments feel personal. The brand should feel warm, thoughtful, modern, celebratory, and human.

Useful positioning phrases:

- Personalized gifts for moments that deserve more than generic.
- Gifts that remember the detail only you would know.
- Made for birthdays, milestones, "just because", and everything in between.
- Thoughtful personalization for the people who matter.

Avoid making the brand sound luxury-only, cheap, gimmicky, overly sentimental, or mass-market generic.

## Voice

Use:

- Warm, clear, and emotionally specific.
- Modern social wording, but not slang-heavy.
- Observational hooks about relationships and gifting behavior.
- Short lines for TikTok, Reels, Shorts, and X.
- Useful, searchable language for Pinterest and website copy.

Avoid:

- Overpromising life-changing emotional impact.
- Guilt-based gifting.
- Excessive urgency unless the user provides a live sale.
- Generic phrases such as "the perfect gift for everyone" unless revised into a more specific angle.
- Claims about delivery dates, discounts, materials, guarantees, or customer ratings unless provided.

## Brand-Safe Emotional Territories

- "I saw this and thought of you."
- Remembering a detail.
- Milestones becoming keepsakes.
- Tiny rituals of care.
- Personalization as proof of attention.
- Gifts that feel made for one person, not a crowd.

## Product Tie-In Rule

For brand-first content, connect the product softly:

- Lead with the human moment.
- Show the relationship or occasion.
- Introduce the personalized gift as the object that carries the meaning.
- End with a gentle CTA.

Example:

Instead of: "Buy our personalized necklace now."

Use: "For the friend who remembers every detail, give one back: a personalized piece made around her name, date, or story."
